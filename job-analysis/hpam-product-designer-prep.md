# Interview Prep Report: PT Henan Putihrai Asset Management (HPAM)
**Role:** Product Designer
**Company Context:** PT Henan Putihrai Asset Management (HPAM) is a licensed, renowned local Indonesian Investment Manager established in 2006. They handle various mutual funds (Reksa Dana Saham, Pendapatan Tetap, dll).
**Core Focus of the Role:** Shaping UX/UI for trading platforms, digital onboarding, and internal ERP/workflow tools.

---

## 🎯 1. Initial Screen: Core Behavioral Questions & "The Elevator Pitch"

**Q: Tell me about yourself and your background as a Product Designer.**
* **Your Goal:** Show that you are a seasoned designer who understands both user experience and business operations.
* **Suggested Answer:** "I am a Product Designer with a strong track record of simplifying complex systems into intuitive experiences. My career spans across high-transaction ecosystems, most notably at STOQO and GudangAda, where I built B2B platforms and internal operations tools (like warehouse management and logistics tracking). I specialize in translating heavy workflows into 'chewable' interfaces, whether that's routing trucks or modeling onboarding flows. Right now, I'm looking to bring my expertise in scalable design systems and operational efficiency to the fintech space, which is why I was so drawn to HPAM's needs for their trading and ERP platforms."

**Q: Why are you interested in joining Henan Putihrai Asset Management (HPAM)?**
* **Your Goal:** Show that you understand what they do (Mutual Funds/Investment Manager) and tie it to your desire to build trust-based products.
* **Suggested Answer:** "I've always believed that the best products are those that manage risk and build trust. HPAM has been a recognized Investment Manager since 2006, and as you digitize your onboarding and trading platforms, the design challenge revolves entirely around financial trust. My experience is heavily rooted in designing mission-critical systems where a UX mistake can ruin an operation. I want to take my experience designing high-stakes B2B infrastructure and apply it to HPAM’s ecosystem to help everyday investors feel secure and empowered when navigating your mutual funds."

**Q: Why are you leaving your current role / What are you looking for in your next role?**
* **Suggested Answer:** "I'm looking for an environment where I can own the end-to-end design lifecycle of a complex product ecosystem. In my previous roles, I've successfully built design systems from scratch (like GudangAda) and digitized entire field operations (like STOQO). I am looking for a new challenge in an industry that demands high precision, like financial services and asset management. HPAM presents the perfect opportunity to bridge complex financial workflows with clean, scalable user interfaces."

## 🤝 2. Collaboration & Team Fit (Crucial for HR)

**Q: How do you handle working with cross-functional teams, specifically Product Managers and Engineers?**
* **Your Goal:** Prove you are highly collaborative and don't work in a vacuum.
* **Suggested Answer:** "I never design in a silo. I view Product Managers as my partners in defining the 'What' and the 'Why', while I own the 'How'. With Engineers, I engage them very early in the wireframing phase, not just at handoff. For example, during the STOQO Delivery Logistics project, I led a co-creation workshop alongside the Engineering Lead and Ops Head before opening Figma. This ensures that whatever I design is not just beautiful, but technically feasible and aligned with the backend architecture."

**Q: Tell me about a time you faced pushback on a design or had a disagreement with a stakeholder. How did you resolve it?**
* **Your Goal:** Show maturity, lack of ego, and data-driven decision making.
* **Suggested Answer:** "At STOQO, we had a debate regarding the Sales Team's KPIs dashboard. Stakeholders wanted to pack as much data as possible onto the main screen. However, my field research showed that the sales agents were overwhelmed and confused by the data density. Instead of arguing based on opinion, I brought the stakeholders to a usability testing session. When they watched the agents struggle to find their daily target, the conversation shifted immediately. We compromised by hiding complex metrics behind progressive disclosure and highlighting a 'Chewable' daily goal. I always resolve disagreements by bringing the focus back to objective user research and business goals."

**Q: What is your process when given a very vague requirement from a business team?**
* **Suggested Answer:** "I start by asking identifying questions to uncover the root problem. Vague requirements usually mean the stakeholder knows they have a pain point, but they are prescribing a solution instead of stating the problem. I run a quick discovery session: 'What user behavior are we trying to change?' and 'How will we measure success?'. Once the business goal is clear, I map out the current user flow and create low-fidelity wireframes to quickly align everyone's mental model before spending time on high-fidelity UI."

## 💼 3. Work Process & Execution

**Q: The role requires maintaining and evolving a Design System. Can you talk about your experience with Design Systems?**
* **Your Goal:** Point directly to GudangAda.
* **Suggested Answer:** "I have deep experience with design systems. At GudangAda, I was responsible for evolving our design system that supported a vast web and mobile ecosystem. A design system is more than just a sticker sheet in Figma; it requires strict governance, establishing reusable tokens, and creating UI components that map 1:1 with the engineers' code library (HTML/CSS/React). I ensure consistency by establishing clear documentation so that when the team scales, the brand identity and interaction patterns remain flawless."

**Q: What is your approach to User Research when you have limited time or budget?**
* **Suggested Answer:** "I believe imperfect research is better than no research. If we don’t have weeks for a formal study, I do 'Guerrilla Testing'. At STOQO, instead of formal labs, I literally rode along in the delivery trucks to observe the drivers under real stressors like sunlight and traffic. For an internal ERP at HPAM, I would sit next to your operations or finance teams for just 30 minutes, watch them click through their current system, and note exactly where they use workarounds or get frustrated. Quick, observational research yields massive insights."

## ❓ 4. Questions YOU Should Ask the HR/Recruiter
*Asking strong questions during the initial interview proves you are actively evaluating them as much as they are evaluating you.*

1. **Team Structure:** "Could you tell me a bit about the structure of the current product and engineering teams? Who does this Product Designer role report to directly?"
2. **Immediate Challenges:** "What is the biggest design challenge the team is facing right now? Is it modernizing the legacy ERP, or focusing on the retail trading onboarding?"
3. **Design Maturity:** "How would you describe the design maturity at HPAM? Is design brought in at the strategy phase, or mostly during the execution and UI phase?"
4. **Next Steps:** "What does the rest of the interview process look like for this role?"

---
**💡 HR Phone Screen Cheat Code:** Always sound enthusiastic, maintain a steady pace, and whenever they ask a hypothetical question, anchor your answer in a **Past Experience** using the **STAR Method** (Situation, Task, Action, Result).
